Context / Challenge

The Business Situation

In 2023, sticky.io faced a critical challenge: payment failures were creating massive involuntary churn, costing millions in revenue. Traditional rule-based payment systems couldn't adapt to sophisticated fraud patterns, declined legitimate transactions, and provided poor customer experiences during payment failures.

Why This Problem Mattered

With sticky.io processing over $100M annually in subscription payments, even small improvements in payment success rates translated to massive revenue impact. More critically, involuntary churn was destroying customer relationships‚Äîsubscribers whose payments failed often never returned, regardless of product satisfaction.

Key Constraints

  • Regulatory Compliance: PCI DSS Level 1 and GDPR requirements limiting data access and processing approaches
  • Legacy Infrastructure: Monolithic payment system with 99.9% uptime requirements during transformation
  • Customer Trust: Payment failures had created negative brand perception requiring careful UX redesign
  • Technical Debt: 5+ years of accumulated payment logic making changes risky and time-intensive

The hidden cost of payment failures: Revenue loss, customer churn, and trust erosion

Approach / What I Did

My Role and Leadership Scope

As VP of Product, UX, and Data, I had full P&L accountability for sticky.io's $44M revenue stream. I led a 12-person cross-functional team including data scientists, UX designers, engineers, and payment specialists, reporting directly to the CEO with board-level visibility on transformation outcomes.

Strategic Framework: Trust-First Transformation

I implemented a holistic approach recognizing that payment optimization required both technical excellence and customer experience redesign:

1. AI-Powered Intelligence

Built machine learning models processing 200+ signals to predict optimal payment timing and methods

2. Customer-Centric UX

Redesigned payment failure flows prioritizing transparency and easy resolution paths

3. Data-Driven Optimization

Implemented real-time A/B testing framework for continuous payment experience improvement

Key Actions and Decisions

  • Customer Research Deep-Dive: Conducted 150+ customer interviews to understand payment failure emotional impact
  • AI-First Architecture: Built real-time ML pipeline processing 50M+ daily transaction signals
  • Cross-Functional Integration: Embedded UX researchers directly into engineering sprints for rapid iteration
  • Compliance-by-Design: Architected privacy-preserving ML models meeting strict regulatory requirements

Integrated approach: AI intelligence + Customer experience + Continuous optimization

Solution / Execution

What Was Delivered

The Smart Dunning AI platform combined machine learning payment optimization with empathetic customer experience design. The system predicted optimal retry timing, personalized payment methods, and provided transparent communication during any payment issues.

How It Worked

1. Predictive Payment Intelligence

ML models analyze 200+ signals (payment history, device fingerprinting, behavioral patterns) to predict optimal payment timing and method selection

2. Personalized Retry Logic

AI determines individualized retry schedules based on customer payment patterns, reducing false declines by 67%

3. Transparent Customer Communication

Empathetic messaging and self-service resolution tools maintain trust during payment resolution process

Innovation Highlights

  • Real-Time ML Pipeline: Sub-100ms payment decision processing with continuous model learning
  • Behavioral Payment Prediction: Customer activity patterns predicting payment success probability
  • Privacy-Preserving AI: Federated learning approaches meeting GDPR requirements
  • Empathetic UX Design: Payment failure experiences redesigned for trust and easy resolution

End-to-end Smart Dunning platform: From prediction to customer resolution

  • Operational Efficiency: Automated 85% of payment retry decisions reducing manual intervention

  • Competitive Advantage: Created proprietary ML models providing sustainable differentiation
  • Team Transformation: Established data-driven culture with embedded UX research practices

Lasting Legacy

The Smart Dunning AI platform continues operating as sticky.io's core payment infrastructure, processing millions of transactions monthly. The ML models and UX patterns I developed became the foundation for additional fintech products, with the privacy-preserving AI approach becoming a template for GDPR-compliant ML systems.

Reflection / Learning

What Worked

  • Customer-Centric AI: Starting with customer pain points before technical solutions ensured AI served real needs
  • Integrated UX + AI: Combining machine learning with empathetic design created holistic experience improvement
  • Compliance-First Development: Building privacy protection into ML architecture prevented costly retrofitting
  • Continuous Learning: Real-time model updates and A/B testing enabled rapid optimization post-launch

What Didn't Work

  • Initial Model Complexity: Early AI models were over-engineered; simpler ensemble approaches proved more effective
  • Change Management: Customer success team needed more training on new payment resolution tools
  • Integration Timeline: Legacy system constraints created longer migration timeline than anticipated

How It Shaped My Approach

This experience reinforced my belief that breakthrough product innovation happens at the intersection of advanced technology and deep customer empathy. Pure technical optimization misses emotional dimensions that drive real business outcomes.

The 74% NPS improvement taught me that customer perception of product value often depends more on how technology makes them feel than what it technically accomplishes. I now start every AI/ML project by understanding the emotional journey, not just the technical requirements.

Most importantly, this project demonstrated that sustainable competitive advantage comes from integrated capabilities (AI + UX + compliance) that competitors cannot easily replicate, rather than point solutions that can be copied.
